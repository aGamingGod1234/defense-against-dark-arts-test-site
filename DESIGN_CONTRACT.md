# Design Contract

Reading this as: a cinematic launch page for adult dark-fantasy action players, with original occult-academy worldbuilding and campaign-grade art direction.

Dials: variance 9, motion 7, density 3.

## Color system
Harmony: monochrome with a controlled luminous accent
Base: black-green graphite
Temperature: cool
Background: #070a09
Surface: #101512
Raised surface: #171d19
Primary text: #f0f2e9
Muted text: #a6ada3
Hairline: rgba(240, 242, 233, 0.16)
Accent: #c8ff5a
Accent hover: #d9ff8b
Danger is reserved for actual errors and does not decorate the page.

## Narrative spine
A forbidden codex opens into a combat oath. The visitor moves from threat, to doctrine, to world, to enemy, to protagonist, to action.

## Shape and material rules
- Primary layout uses sharp or very lightly softened 4px geometry.
- Buttons may be pill-shaped only as a documented control exception.
- Matte obsidian surfaces, oxidized silver lines, wet stone imagery, one luminous chartreuse accent.
- Avoid glass cards. The hero nav may use restrained backdrop filtering because it is fixed.

## Section composition ledger
1. Hero: image-as-canvas, title low-left, threat high-right within art.
2. Oath: oversized editorial quotation with vertical rule and interactive seal button.
3. Disciplines: central spell ring with asymmetric text rail, stateful tabs.
4. Hall: full-bleed image, copy pinned to lower band.
5. Dossier: compressed vertical enemy slices on desktop, horizontal snap list on touch.
6. Keeper: portrait left, narrow manuscript panel right, no repeated hero split proportions.
7. Combat and close: cinematic combat crop, copy embedded in a controlled safe area, then a sparse final action.

## Motion
- Hero art breathes by a subtle scroll scale and pointer-weighted light.
- Discipline selection rotates and rewrites the ring, showing a true state change.
- Enemy slices expand with spring mass.
- Section reveals use one-time opacity and transform only.
- No infinite marquee, no custom cursor, no layout-property animation.

## Copy posture
Short declarative lines. Concrete nouns and verbs. No fake numbers, reviews, release dates, platforms, awards, or availability claims.

## Acceptance
The output must feel like a premium game campaign page rather than a fantasy-themed SaaS template. Every major section must have a distinct silhouette. Hero, Hall, combat, and keeper images must remain readable on desktop and mobile. All CTAs clearly belong to a fictional interactive concept.
