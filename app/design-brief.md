# Defense Against the Dark Arts Design Brief

## Objective
Create an original premium dark-fantasy game campaign page that moves from threat, to doctrine, to world, to enemy, to protagonist, to action.

## Motion spec
- Hero art uses restrained scroll depth and pointer light.
- Discipline changes rotate the central instrument and replace panel copy.
- Enemy dossiers expand as a true responsive disclosure state.
- Section reveals use opacity and transform only.
- Reduced motion removes nonessential movement.

## Self-critique before coding
Primary risks were franchise resemblance, black-on-black loss of hierarchy, generic fantasy UI cards, and repeated split-screen sections. The final system uses original terminology, one chartreuse signal color, campaign photography, and seven distinct silhouettes.

## Section distinctiveness ledger
1. Image-as-canvas hero.
2. Oath manifesto with interactive circular seal.
3. Three-column discipline instrument.
4. Full-bleed Hall of Vows environment.
5. Expanding enemy dossier.
6. Editorial character chapter.
7. Full-bleed combat scene with sparse closing action.

## CTA inventory
- `Enter the Collegium` is the primary concept action.
- `Study the disciplines` is the secondary navigation action.
- `Begin the study` repeats the same primary intent only in the closing section.

## Mobile plan
- Navigation condenses to brand mark plus action.
- Oath stacks and keeps the seal touch target large.
- Discipline tabs become three equal controls above the instrument.
- Enemy dossier becomes horizontal snap cards with all details readable.
- Keeper portrait moves above the copy.
- Hero and combat crops preserve the actor and threat.

## Performance gate
Seven WebP campaign assets plus favicon and app icons remain below 2.5 MiB. All images have intrinsic dimensions or fill reserved layouts.
